Super Juice

How to optimize your citrus by wasting less and tasting more

Ever wrapped a prep shift, looked down at a trashcan full to the brim with the carcasses of pressed citrus, and pondered your own mortality wondered if there's a more sustainable way to run a bar? If so, you're not alone, and the good news is there's a better way! Thanks to modern science and bartender/genuine Kentucky Colonel Nickle Morris, you can get up to 8x the yield with Super Juice - an enhanced recipe for citrus that tastes just as good on day 7 as it does on day 1.

With a little help from powdered citric and malic acids, we can slow down the usual oxidation that instantly starts degrading fresh juice on its own. The process starts by peeling your citrus with as little white pith as possible, weighing the peels, and putting them in a jar (big enough to accommodate a stick blender if you've got one) with the appropriate amount of citric (and malic for lime and orange) acid. Then, shake the jar and let it rest for up to 2 hours. This creates "oleo citrate", which is like the acidified sibling of the "oleo saccharum" created when macerating fruit with sugar.

Next, pour everything from the jar into a blender and add water (see table for specs), being sure to rinse the jar with your measured water to get all of that good oleo citrate out. Or just add water to your jar if using a stick blender. Then, juice your citrus and add it to the mix. Blend for ~30 seconds, strain through a milk bag or cheesecloth and boom, you've got Super Juice. It's darned close to shelf stable and will taste like freshly squeezed juice for 2 weeks!

What you'll need:

  • Citrus (8 limes yields 4+ cups of juice!)
  • Citric Acid
  • Malic Acid
  • Digital scale
  • Blender (stick preferred to avoid heat)
  • Fine strainer (nut milk bag or cheesecloth)

Super Juice Specs:

*Multiply amounts by the weight of your peels
Fruit Citric Acid Malic Acid Water
Lime 0.66 0.33 16.66
Lemon 1.0 (equal) none 16.66
Orange 0.9 0.1 16.66
